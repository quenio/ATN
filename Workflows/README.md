# ATN Workflows

Using ATN to organize related activities into repeatable paths of work.

## [Assurance](./Assurance)

A workflow for checking consistency between intent, realization, and observed behavior.

## [Engineering](./Engineering)

A workflow for refining specifications into implementable structures and building from them.

## [Operations](./Operations)

A workflow for defining intended action, exploring scenarios, executing under constraints, and observing outcomes.

## [Requirements](./Requirements)

A workflow for turning domain understanding into precise, testable constraints.

## Sources

The workflow names and activity groupings above are aligned with common systems engineering and life-cycle terminology, as corroborated by the following sources:

- [NASA Systems Engineering Handbook](https://www.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/nasa_systems_engineering_handbook_0.pdf), which contributed the distinction between requirements definition, design solution definition, implementation, integration, verification, validation, and operations as related but separable process areas.
- [DoD Systems Engineering Guidebook](https://www.cto.mil/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/SE-Guidebook-Feb2022.pdf), which contributed the framing of stakeholder requirements, requirements analysis, architecture design, implementation, integration, verification, validation, transition, and technical reviews as coordinated life-cycle processes.
- [SEBoK: Applying Life Cycle Processes](https://sebokwiki.org/wiki/Applying_Life_Cycle_Processes), which contributed the emphasis on concurrency, iteration, recursion, and the relationships among requirements, architecture, integration, verification, validation, operation, and maintenance when grouping activities into workflows.
